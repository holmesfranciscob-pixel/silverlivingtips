Simple, low‑cost improvements

1) Brighter Task Lighting

Why: Reduce eye strain and improve visibility for cooking, reading, and hobbies.

How: Add motion‑sensor under‑cabinet strips or brighter LED bulbs (3000–4000K).

2) Anti‑Slip Bathroom Mats

Why: Wet floors are slippery; textured mats with beveled edges add grip.

How: Use rubber‑backed mats and keep pathways clear of loose rugs.

3) Grab Bars That Look Like Towel Rails

Why: Subtle safety that blends in aesthetically.

How: Install into studs near showers and toilets; follow manufacturer torque specs.

4) Lever‑Style Door Handles

Why: Easier on wrists and fingers than round knobs.

How: Swap common interior knobs for levers—usually a 10‑minute job each.

5) Soft‑Close Seats & Drawers

Why: Less bending, less noise, fewer pinched fingers.

How: Upgrade hinges/slides or use soft‑close add‑on dampers.

6) High‑Contrast Pantry Labels

Why: Faster find, less strain under lower light.

How: Large fonts (18–24pt), dark type on light background; label shelves & bins.

7) ComfortLift Cushion Editor’s Pick

Why: A firm, supportive cushion can reduce knee/hip strain when standing from chairs.

Who benefits: Anyone who feels a “dip” in low chairs; avoid if your clinician advises otherwise.
